Biography

John Lautner was an American architect whose work spanned five decades of bold experimentation with structure, space, and natural integration. A student of Frank Lloyd Wright at Taliesin, Lautner developed an approach that emphasized the relationship between architecture and its site, often creating structures that seemed to defy conventional building logic.

His residential projects in Los Angeles and Palm Springs became icons of mid-century modernism, characterized by dramatic cantilevers, concrete shells, and glass walls that dissolved the boundary between interior and landscape. Lautner rejected stylistic labels, believing each building should emerge from its unique conditions.

His work has been featured in numerous films and publications, cementing his legacy as one of the most visionary architects of the twentieth century. The Sheats-Goldstein Residence was donated to LACMA in 2016 to serve as a museum space.

Philosophy

"Architecture should be human, dynamic, and free. Each space is a unique solution to the human experience of living."

— John Lautner
