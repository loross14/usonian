Architectural Overview

The Chemosphere is an octagon-shaped house perched atop a concrete column, designed to address the near-impossible 45-degree slope of the site. Commissioned by Leonard Malin, an aerospace engineer, the residence exemplifies Lautner's engineering ingenuity and his refusal to be constrained by conventional building methods.


The structure rises 29 feet above the hillside, accessed originally by funicular railway. The octagonal form maximizes floor space while minimizing the structural footprint. Floor-to-ceiling windows provide 360-degree views of the San Fernando Valley. The residence has been featured in numerous films, most notably "Body Double" (1984).
