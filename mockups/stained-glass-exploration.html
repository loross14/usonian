Robie Chevron Transom

Agent Learnings · Design 01
Pattern Analysis

The Robie House windows feature bold chevron/arrow motifs that create dynamic horizontal movement. The central spine uses mirrored V-shapes that radiate outward, creating visual tension and energy. Square corner elements anchor the composition and provide geometric stability.

Color Extraction

Primary palette is warm amber (#C4A84B), cream (#F5F0E6), and deep brown (#8B4513). The zinc caming appears as charcoal (#2C2C2C). These earth tones evoke the Prairie landscape and provide warmth when backlit.

Design Principles

Strong horizontal emphasis echoes Prairie architecture. Symmetry provides balance while chevrons add dynamism. Nested geometric forms create depth. The design celebrates the interplay of light and lead lines.

10. March Balloons (Coonley)

IMAGE LOADING ERROR

Source: Maclin Studio

Adaptation · Our Design

Celebration Balloons Transom

Agent Learnings · Design 10
Pattern Analysis

The Coonley Playhouse windows are Wright's most playful designs. Circles represent balloons rising on curving strings. Unlike his usual geometric abstraction, these windows embrace recognizable, childlike imagery. Asymmetrical placement creates dynamic, celebratory movement.

Color Extraction

Bold primary and secondary colors: red (#E63946), blue (#457B9D), yellow/orange (#F4A261, #F77F00), green (#2A9D8F), purple (#9B5DE5). Cream background (#FFFEF5) and black caming provide unity. Joyful, saturated palette for children's space.

Design Principles

Circles as primary form (unusual for FLW). Varied sizes create depth and rhythm. Curved strings connect forms to ground. Flag/pennant accents add geometric counterpoint. Design demonstrates FLW could adapt his language for context—here, a playhouse for children.

20. Heath House Window

WILLIAM HEATH HOUSE · BUFFALO · 1905

Mature Prairie period design

Adaptation · Our Design

Heath Horizontal Transom

Agent Learnings · Design 20
Pattern Analysis

Five primary horizontal bands at varying thicknesses (6px, 4px, 12px, 4px, 6px) create A-B-C-B-A rhythm. Four equal sections (80px each). Small squares arranged in mirrored pairs above and below center band. Diamond motifs embedded in central amber band.

Color Extraction

Amber light (#E8A832), amber dark (#C78522), gold (#C9A227, #DDB832), saddle brown (#8B5A2B), cream (#FDF8E8, #F5EED6), lead dark (#2C2418).

Design Principles

Unity through repetition at consistent intervals. Asymmetry within symmetry - color distribution varies subtly. Hierarchy of weight - thicker bands anchor, medium bands frame, thin bands provide structure. Light as material - gradients simulate luminous quality of real stained glass.

Cumulative Design Principles
Geometric Vocabulary

FLW's "Grammar of the Straight Line" uses rectangles, squares, triangles, diamonds, and chevrons. Nature is "conventionalized" - reduced to geometric essence while maintaining recognizable character. Bilateral symmetry provides stability. Horizontal emphasis echoes prairie landscape.

Color Philosophy

Earth-tone palettes: amber, gold, sage green, brown, cream, copper. Warm colors transform harsh sunlight into welcoming illumination. Cool blues used sparingly as sky accents. Iridescent/opalescent effects create depth. Lead caming as dark brown/bronze patina.

Structural Integration

Lead lines (zinc caming) celebrated as design element, not hidden. Corner anchoring with geometric accents. Central focal points with radiating or flanking elements. Modular repetition enables scalability. Light as building material - designs account for transmitted light.
