OFFICIAL DESIGNATION SYSTEM

"Preservation Tiers"

CRITERIA FOR ARCHITECTURAL SIGNIFICANCE

★ TIER I ★

LANDMARK

Properties of exceptional national significance. Architectural works that have profoundly influenced the course of American design history.

  • Direct association with master architect
  • Exceptional integrity of original design
  • Documented historical significance
  • Public accessibility provisions
TIER II

CONTRIBUTING STRUCTURE

Properties that contribute to the character of a historic district. Works that embody distinctive characteristics of a period or method.

  • Designed by recognized architect
  • Substantial original fabric retained
  • Compatible alterations documented
  • Maintenance covenant in place
TIER III

ELIGIBLE

Properties that meet the criteria for listing but have not yet been formally nominated. Candidates for future preservation action.

  • Meets age threshold (50+ years)
  • Architectural merit evident
  • Research documentation pending
  • Owner consent not yet obtained
