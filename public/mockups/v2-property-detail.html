THE STORY

Perched 150 feet above the ground on a single concrete column, the Chemosphere represents one of the most daring architectural achievements of the 20th century. John Lautner designed this octagonal residence when the steep hillside lot was deemed unbuildable by conventional standards.

The house became an instant icon, appearing in films and establishing Lautner as a visionary who refused to accept limitations. Its flying saucer-like form continues to captivate architects and enthusiasts alike, standing as a testament to bold imagination and engineering prowess.

Completely restored to its original glory while incorporating modern systems, the Chemosphere offers a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to own a piece of architectural history that exists nowhere else on Earth.
